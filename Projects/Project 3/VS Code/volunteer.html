    • Open doors with your heart

    Volunteers are an integral part of the healing process employed by HHH. They forge new connections with children from broken families and help them learn to trust again. They also encourage them to extend this trust to the world.


    You can be a part of this process by joining our Volunteer team!

    Volunteer Opportunities

    1. Homework Helpers - Assist children with schoolwork and help develop academic skills, such as reading and writing.
    2. Bedtime Readers - Help children feel safe and secure at bedtime by reading stories to them as they fall asleep.
    3. Volunteer Groups - Facilitate special events and activities on campus for the children, such as carnivals, field days, arts and crafts projects, sporting events, performances and workshops.
    4. Activities Volunteer - Play with the children during after-school hours, 3:30 p.m. to 5:00 p.m., and on weekends. You will be trained in a number of playground games you may use, and you will also assist with any activities that are happening at the Home.
    5. Group Service Opportunities - Group or individual volunteers may also perform hand on service (wash vans, bike maintenance, grounds beautification, inventory, library organization, etc.), organize a drive (see Wish List for more info), or sponsor an activity for our kids within the community (trip, performance, baseball game, circus, museum, movie, etc).
    6. Wind Down Helpers - Support the homes during this mellow time of day by helping prepare for bed times, eat dinner, straighten the home, do bed time routines, etc.

    Steps To Becoming A Volunteer

    1. Commit to at least one hour a week of service for a minimum of six months (NOTE: Your 6 months of service do not have to be consecutive and you are NOT limited to 6 months)
    2. Fill out and submit a volunteer application (online, via email, or by US Mail)
    3. Attend one of the two available Volunteer Information Sessions as arranged by the Volunteer Manager.
    4. At the information session, the Volunteer Manager will schedule an interview with you. Please bring any calendar information you might have to this session to help facilitate this process.
    5. After your interview, you will complete a background check, drug screening and Tuberculosis testing.
    6. Attend one of the two available Volunteer Orientations as arranged by the Volunteer Manager.

    Once all testing has been cleared, you will be given a log-in number and home assignment.>


    Helping Hand Home does not fulfill court-ordered community service requests.

    All volunteers must meet the minimum age requirement of 18 years of age. Any applications whose applicants do not meet this requirement will be immediately disqualified.
